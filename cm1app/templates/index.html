a bunch of sensors out in different sites... sometimes sensors go bad... this is the system status at a glance. green means sensors is in good health (got fresh and valid data from them); amber means some readings are out of reasonable range; red means communication to sensor is lost

As part of an NSF-funded project at the University of Hawaii at Manoa, an array of custom data loggers are installed at the He'eia fishpond in Oahu, Hawaii. The goals of the project are:

  • To study the biogeochemistry of coastal environment
  • To evaluate the performance of low-cost MEMS sensors and single-board computers as alternative to commercial solutions for in-situ monitoring
  • To develop a low-maintenance sensor system suitable for K-12 STEM education
  • To provide real-time water quality data to the staff at Paepae o He'eia

Live data can be found here. Technical details of the monitoring system can be found here.

Dr. Brian T. Glazer is the Principal Investigator of the project. For more information, visit

  • Dr. Glazer's project webpage
  • He'eia Coastal Ocean Observing System
  • Project info at Paepae o He'eia
